Choosing a model

Fast vs. capable — pick per task, not once

Simple task → Haiku. Everyday work → Sonnet. Hard problem → Opus.

Haiku
Fastest — quick lookups, simple rewrites, basic answers.
Sonnet
The default — solid for most day-to-day work: drafting, analysis, coding.
Opus
Most capable — worth it for genuinely hard or high-stakes problems.
Fable
Anthropic's newest tier, alongside Opus.
Switch models per conversation from the picker in the chat box.

Handle with judgment

Three settings to never turn on

01
Never use --dangerously-skip-permissions in Claude CodeNo exceptions — it removes every safety check at once.
02
Don't turn on Cowork's "Skip all approvals" modeThe current name for what used to be called "Act without asking."
03
Don't enable "Always allow" for connector toolsThat setting removes the per-action approval gate that's the actual security control.
Handle with judgment

Three habits to keep, regardless

01
Review before anything irreversibleSending, deleting, publishing — check the step before it happens.
02
Never hand it credentials directlyPasswords, keys, and tokens stay out of the conversation.
03
Know what's connected before you ask it to actIf it can touch a system, know which one and why.
